A little about me

Greetings! My name is Peter Wells and I am a graduate student in the Computer Science program at Portland State University. I began my journey here as a post-baccalaureate student to learn how to write great software. After working my way through the required undergrad, I've now began the master's program in hopes of gaining the knowledge that can help me pursue a career in software development.

Aside from school, I am now participating in an internship with Northwest Evaluation Association (NWEA) as a back-end software developer. NWEA is a national company that helps evaluate student's progress in various subjects in school and generate reports to help guide their education. This is an issue I am passionate about as I believe there is a lot more we can be doing to promote education nationally. I have just began there, but the type of projects I will be working on involve migrating services to reduce overhead cost of operating such a large database and I am excited to work on an enterprise level project.

Outside of work, you can find me participating in the Portland music scene in a variety of ways. I like to make music at home, play drums in local bands across town and have even worked as an audio engineer in the past. Beyond that, I am an avid gamer and plan on making some games of my own as well.

I believe that software can be a powerful tool to enrich people's lives and help guide us to solutions to problems that may be otherwise impossible to discover. To that end, I have always had an interest in learning how software like this can be built, how it can interact with us in our lives, and am excited for the future.
